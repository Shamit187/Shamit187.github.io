Distill Agent

DistilAgent is a framework designed to improve the autonomous UI navigation capabilities of smaller open-source language models (LLMs). In this work, we address the limitations of smaller models in handling long-horizon tasks by incorporating three components: task discretization, explicit reasoning enforcement, and exploration encouragement. DistilAgent significantly boosts task completion rates, demonstrating success by improving GPT-4's rate from 50% to 90% and enhancing LLaMA-3-8B from 0% to 60% after fine-tuning with the AutoNav1K dataset we collected. This approach democratizes access to high-performance autonomous LLM agents.

Map GPT

Spatial problems, known for their complexity, present significant challenges for algorithmic solutions. Traditional machine learning (ML) models require specialized training for each unique scenario and often underperform in generating novel paths from historical data. In this study, we propose leveraging large language models (LLMs), utilizing diverse prompting techniques to produce viable paths. Through experiments with both GPT-4 and LLaMA3 8B, we demonstrate that LLM-based methods outperform conventional approaches, offering a flexible and efficient framework for solving complex spatial problems.
